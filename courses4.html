Introduction

Conflict is a natural and inevitable part of human interaction, but how we choose to manage it can make all the difference. Conflict management requires a willingness to listen, understand, and empathize with others, while also being assertive and standing up for oneself. It involves finding a mutually beneficial solution rather than seeking to "win" the argument. When we approach conflict with a mindset of openness, curiosity, and respect, we create the opportunity for growth, learning, and deeper connections with others. True conflict management isn't about avoiding or suppressing conflict, but about embracing it as a chance to transform ourselves and our relationships. It involves finding a mutually beneficial solution rather than seeking to "win" the argument.
