      Instructions

      Active listening involves being fully present and engaged in the conversation, with the intention of understanding the speaker's perspective. To improve your active listening skills, focus on being present in the moment, maintaining eye contact, and avoiding distractions.

      To overcome these barriers, try to focus your attention solely on the speaker, eliminate distractions, and let go of preconceptions or assumptions. Engage in active listening by paying attention to what the speaker is saying, summarizing their points, and asking clarifying questions.

      Reflective listening involves summarizing what the speaker has said in your own words to demonstrate that you understand and have been actively listening. It's important to avoid interrupting the speaker or offering advice until they have finished speaking.

      Active listening is an important skill to develop, as it shows the speaker that you value their perspective and are interested in understanding their point of view. This can help build trust, strengthen relationships, and minimize the potential for conflicts

      Reflecting, paraphrasing, and summarizing are all techniques that can help demonstrate active listening and show the speaker that you are engaged and interested in what they have to say. Avoid interrupting or finishing their sentences, as this can be disrespectful and disruptive.
